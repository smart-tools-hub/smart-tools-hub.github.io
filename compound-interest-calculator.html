What is Compound Interest and Why It Matters

Compound interest is the interest calculated on your initial principal plus the accumulated interest from previous periods. This is also called "interest on interest" and it makes your money grow exponentially over time. Unlike simple interest, compound interest rewards you for keeping money invested longer. Albert Einstein called compound interest the "eighth wonder of the world" because of its power to multiply wealth. How Compounding Frequency Affects Your Returns

Compounding frequency makes a big difference in your final returns. Monthly compounding gives higher returns than yearly compounding because interest gets added to principal more often. For example, ₹1 lakh invested at 10% for 10 years becomes ₹2.59 lakh with yearly compounding, but ₹2.70 lakh with monthly compounding. Daily compounding gives the maximum returns. This is why banks and mutual funds prefer monthly or daily compounding. Frequently Asked Questions About Compound Interest

Q1: What is the compound interest formula
A: The formula is A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt) where A is final amount, P is principal, r is annual interest rate, n is compounding frequency per year, and t is time in years. Our calculator does this math automatically for you.

Q2: Is compound interest better than simple interest
A: Yes, compound interest always gives higher returns than simple interest for periods longer than 1 year. The longer you invest, the bigger the difference becomes because interest keeps earning interest in compound interest.

Q3: Which compounding frequency is best for savings
A: Daily or monthly compounding is best because interest gets added to your principal more frequently. Most Indian banks offer quarterly compounding on FD, while mutual funds compound daily, giving you better returns over time.

A: Use our free calculator above. Enter principal, annual rate, years, and select "Monthly" as compounding frequency. It will show your future value and total interest earned without any complex manual calculation.
