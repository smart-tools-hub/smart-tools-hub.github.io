What Is Citation and Why Is It Important In Academic Writing?

Citation is the process of giving credit to the original author of information, ideas or quotes that you use in your work. Proper citation helps you avoid plagiarism and makes your research paper more credible and trustworthy.

There are 4 main citation styles used worldwide. APA is used in Psychology, Education and Social Sciences. MLA is used in Literature, Arts and Humanities. Chicago is used in History and Publishing. Harvard is popular in UK and Australia universities.

Google Scholar and universities also check for proper citations. If you don't cite sources, your paper can be rejected. With our free citation generator tool online, you can generate 100+ citations in 5 minutes with 100% accuracy.

Supported Citation Styles and Source Types

APA 7th Edition: Most used in Psychology, Nursing, Business and Social Science. Format: Author, A. A. (Year). Title. Publisher. URL

MLA 9th Edition: Used by English Literature and Arts students. Format: Author Last Name, First Name. "Title." Publisher, Year. URL

Chicago Style: Used by History and Book authors. Supports both Notes-Bibliography and Author-Date system.

Harvard Style: Author-Date system. Popular in UK and Australia. Format: Author, Year. Title. Publisher. Available at: URL

Source Types: Website, Book, Journal Article, YouTube Video, Magazine, Newspaper and more coming soon.
