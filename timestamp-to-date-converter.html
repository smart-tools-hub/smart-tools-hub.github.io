What is Unix Timestamp?

Unix timestamp is the number of seconds or milliseconds that have elapsed since January 1, 1970 00:00:00 UTC. Developers use timestamps to store date and time in databases because they are simple numbers and easy to sort. If you are a programmer, data analyst, or web developer, you often need to convert Unix time to human readable date format.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the difference between timestamp in seconds and milliseconds?
A: Unix timestamp in seconds is 10 digits like 1728096000. Milliseconds timestamp is 13 digits like 1728096000000. JavaScript uses milliseconds while many databases use seconds.
Q: What is the starting point of Unix timestamp?
A: Unix timestamp starts from January 1, 1970 00:00:00 UTC. This is called Unix epoch time.
