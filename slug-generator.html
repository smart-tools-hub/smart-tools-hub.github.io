What is a URL Slug and Why It Matters for SEO

A URL slug is the readable part of a web address that comes after your domain name. For example, in smart-tools-hub.github.io/slug-generator, the word "slug-generator" is the slug. Google uses slugs to understand what your page is about, so making them SEO friendly directly impacts your search rankings. A good slug is short, uses only lowercase letters, replaces spaces with hyphens, and avoids special characters. Instead of my%20blog%20post%202025!, you should use my-blog-post-2025. This simple change makes your URL clean, readable, and keyword rich for both users and search engines.

How to Create Perfect URL Slugs for Better Rankings

Creating SEO friendly slugs is one of the easiest on-page SEO tasks with the biggest impact. First, keep your slug under 60 characters and include your main keyword naturally. Second, use hyphens to separate words because Google treats hyphens as space separators, while underscores are not recommended. Third, avoid stop words like "the", "and", "of" if your slug gets too long. Fourth, always use lowercase letters because URLs are case sensitive on some servers. Frequently Asked Questions About URL Slugs

Q1: What is the best length for a URL slug in 2025
A: Keep your slug between 3 to 5 words or under 60 characters. Short slugs load faster, look cleaner in search results, and have higher click through rates. Google recommends descriptive but concise URLs for better SEO performance.

Q2: Should I include keywords in my slug
A: Yes, but naturally. Include your primary keyword once in the slug without keyword stuffing. Example: If your post is about "BMI Calculator", use bmi-calculator not best-bmi-calculator-free-online-tool. Natural keywords help Google understand your content topic.

Q3: Can I change a slug after publishing my blog post
A: Yes, but use 301 redirect from old slug to new slug. Changing slugs without redirect can cause 404 errors and lose SEO ranking. WordPress and Blogger both allow slug editing, but always set up a redirect to protect your traffic and backlinks.

Q4: Hyphen or underscore which is better for slugs
A: Always use hyphens. Google confirmed that hyphens are treated as word separators, while underscores connect words together. So seo-tips is read as "seo tips" but seo_tips is read as "seotips" which hurts your SEO.
